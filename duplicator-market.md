<p><p>Duplicator markets can be categorized into two main types: mechanical and digital duplicators. Mechanical duplicators, often referred to as mimeographs, use physical methods to replicate documents, making them cost-effective for high-volume printing. In contrast, digital duplicators leverage digital technology to produce high-quality prints with quicker turnaround times, often incorporating features such as color printing and user-friendly interfaces. Both types serve niche markets in education, business, and publishing, catering to varying needs for efficiency and print quality.</p></p>
