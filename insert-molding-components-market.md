<p><p>Insert molding involves embedding various materials, such as metal, glass, and rubber, within molded plastic components during the manufacturing process. Metal inserts enhance strength and provide electrical conductivity, while glass inserts improve structural integrity and thermal resistance. Rubber inserts offer flexibility and vibration damping, crucial for applications requiring shock absorption. Other inserts can include ceramics or composites, expanding the functionality of the molded part. This technology enables the creation of complex components with improved performance and durability.</p></p>
