<p><p>Potassium sulfate (CAS 7778-80-5) is produced through various methods catering to different market needs. The Mannheim process involves the reaction of potassium chloride with sulfuric acid, yielding high-purity potassium sulfate. Muriate of Potash (MOP) provides an alternative source from potassium chloride, while the Kieserite process utilizes magnesium sulfate to produce potassium sulfate. Additionally, brines from salt lakes are processed to extract potassium sulfate, leveraging natural sources. These production methods cater to diverse agricultural and industrial applications.</p></p>
